Eric Burger Web Portfolio v1.0 - Class Project

The previous design of my portfolio website involved consideration of design principles and the implementation involved my first experience using Foundation, an HTML/CSS framework.

The prototype for the site includes more features that address requirements for the final project, which involved utilizing process and creation design principles. I produced the following analysis as a way to provide an insight into my design process.

The problem I faced with this project was designing a clean, professional, and responsive web page that showcases my featured projects and successfully highlights my past accomplishments. In developing a solution, I drew up the following objectives:

  • Focus on presentation & visuals
  • Design must emphasize ease of navigation
  • Maintain the user’s interest and attention on content/projects

I incorporated design elements that I used in past projects from other design classes to form a general idea of what I wanted the layout to look like. I made a few rough sketches, but the main pre-visualization work came from what I developed using Axure.

For the design of this project I explored a combination of different design principles. For instance, I wanted to make sure that I was paying attention to alignment at all times. I focused on the relationships between elements and how they related to each other on the space of the page. I wanted to demonstrate this principle on every page, though I found myself focusing primarily on alignment when designing the home page. I also utilized the principle of similarity to create uniformity in elements that I wanted the user to implicitly group together (such as the ‘cards’ for each on project on the ‘Project’ parent page). This resulted in a simple and somewhat repetitive visual design, but I chose to take that design direction to generate consistency and hopefully improve the ease of navigation for users.

Wayfinding was another principle I wanted to incorporate into this design. It is especially pertinent to the site’s purpose to hold the user’s attention for long enough to read about me or view my projects before they navigate away from the site, so I wanted the design to facilitate a certain navigation flow. I organized the links on the nav bar from left to right in a specific sequence, and certain content links to other pages that lead up to the contact section, as outlined below.

Later on in the design process (after drawing up the wireframe), I wanted to introduce another principle--Layering--to manage the amount of content on each page and the sheer number of pages needed to grant each of my projects their own dedicated areas. I solved this problem by using tabs in each Project section of the site, and using project "cards" that link to a project's page. I realized that this also made navigation between projects much easier.

Another part of this design that I feel is particularly effective is my implementation of Consistency.I wanted to adhere to a consistent aesthetic with the site, so I chose to stick with using a few different shades of light blue to maintain a simple visual design. The use of this principle improves learnability of the site's layout and thus improves the process of navigating through areas of the site. The goal of keeping the colors at a minimum was to make sure that I could establish a solid foundation for each page’s skeletal structure before deciding on visuals. I also wanted to emphasize the feeling of simplicity to facilitate ease of usability, in keeping with the Aesthetic-usability effect. There is a trade-off here in some respects: As a result of focusing on showcasing projects and content, it is not the most visually sophisticated design. Instead, I chose to focus on simplicity, similarity, and consistency in favor of a (hopefully) streamlined and straightforward browsing experience for the user.
